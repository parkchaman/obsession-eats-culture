# Culture Eats Strategy for Breakfast. Obsession Eats Culture for Lunch.

Peter Drucker is credited with *"culture eats strategy for breakfast"* — a plan loses to what people actually do. I'm extending it.

**Obsession eats culture for lunch.**

Culture tells people how to act. Obsession makes them unable to act any other way. Culture is external — shared norms a group has to keep maintaining. Obsession is internal — it doesn't need maintaining, and it doesn't quit when the room empties. Strategy is what you *intend*. Culture is how the group *behaves*. Obsession is the one person who *can't behave any other way*. Each beats the one before it because it's more durable and harder to fake.

— Terry Forsyth, June 3, 2026
